Steve look mechanics

Best natural motion:
Steve is a blocky humanoid with a rigid square head, simple eyes, and a stable planted lower body. His look directions should read through small eye shifts first, then subtle head turn or head pitch, then a restrained upper-torso and shoulder follow-through. Keep the feet, hips, and overall body scale anchored. Do not rotate or tilt the whole sprite like a flat card, and do not squash or stretch the skull or body.

What stays anchored:
- Feet stay planted on a shared baseline.
- Hips and lower torso stay close to center with only slight follow-through.
- Overall body height and silhouette mass stay nearly constant.

What leads and follows:
- Eyes lead the gaze with visible pupil and eyelid participation.
- Head follows with a small natural turn for left and right, or a small pitch for up and down.
- Shoulders and upper torso follow slightly behind the head so the pose feels alive but restrained.
- Arms can shift subtly with the torso, but they should not become the main direction cue.

Cardinal pose families:
- 000 up: eyes lifted, slight upward head pitch, chin slightly raised, torso nearly front-facing.
- 090 screen-right: eyes to screen-right, head turned clearly to screen-right, right side of face slightly more visible, shoulders follow a little.
- 180 down: eyes lowered, slight downward head pitch, chin tucked a bit, torso still mostly front-facing.
- 270 screen-left: eyes to screen-left, head turned clearly to screen-left, left side of face slightly more visible, shoulders follow a little.

Diagonal interpolation:
- 022.5, 045, 067.5 step evenly from up toward screen-right.
- 112.5, 135, 157.5 step evenly from screen-right toward down.
- 202.5, 225, 247.5 step evenly from down toward screen-left.
- 292.5, 315, 337.5 step evenly from screen-left toward up.

Motion budget:
- Each 22.5-degree step should advance eyes, head angle, and shoulder follow-through by a similar small amount.
- No adjacent pair should jump in scale, baseline, or body registration.
- Keep Steve unmistakably the same pet in every direction, with no new props, no whole-sprite rotation, and no front-facing drift in the left or right cardinals.
